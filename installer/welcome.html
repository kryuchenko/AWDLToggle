AWDL Toggle fixes a common slow / unstable Wi-Fi issue on MacBook M1/M2/M3 caused by AWDL (Apple Wireless Direct Link) — the networking interface behind AirDrop, AirPlay, Sidecar, Universal Control, Handoff, Universal Clipboard, and Auto Unlock.

On Apple Silicon, macOS uses the same Wi-Fi radio for your normal internet connection and AWDL. When AWDL is active, the radio may periodically jump to AWDL "social" channels to discover nearby Apple devices — typically:

  • 2.4 GHz: Channel 6
  • 5 GHz: Channel 44 (EU) or Channel 149 (US/Canada)

This channel hopping can introduce ping spikes (jitter) and reduce real-world throughput — for example, you might see ~700–800 Mbps with AWDL off vs ~400–500 Mbps with AWDL on (results depend on your router, region, and environment).

With one click you can switch between:

  • Fast Wi-Fi mode — AWDL disabled: lower ping, more stable Wi-Fi, faster downloads
  • AirDrop mode — AWDL enabled: all Continuity features work normally

The app lives in the menu bar, shows the current AWDL status, and toggles instantly.

Note: Speed improvements are usually noticeable only on fast connections (typically above ~300–500 Mbps). On slower internet, the main benefit is often better latency and stability, not higher peak speed. Disabling AWDL turns off the Apple features listed above until you re-enable it.
